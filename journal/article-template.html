How I use AI to improve digital work

I do not see AI as a shortcut. I use it as a way to structure thinking, reduce repetitive work, improve execution and make better use of data in day-to-day digital environments.

By Nada Iddouch · 8 July 2026 · 5 min read

Why this matters

In many teams, digital work is still slowed down by fragmented tools, repeated manual tasks and information that is difficult to reuse. What interests me is not AI for image or trend value, but AI as a practical layer that can make work clearer, faster and more reliable.

My context

My background combines e-commerce, SEO, digital performance, reporting and process thinking. Because of that, I naturally look at work through two lenses at the same time: business value and operational efficiency.

How I use it

I use AI to support research, content preparation, workflow design, documentation and decision support. In practice, this can mean building prompts that create better structure, testing micro-automations, improving repetitive digital tasks or helping transform scattered information into something more usable.

What I pay attention to

Using AI well is not only about generating outputs. It requires judgment, verification, context and a clear understanding of what should remain human. The value does not come from the tool alone, but from the way it is integrated into real work.

What I am learning

The more I work with AI, the more I see that the real skill is not only technical. It is also the ability to ask better questions, define a better framework and connect tools to actual business needs. That is where automation becomes useful instead of superficial.

Next

I want to keep documenting practical uses of AI in digital work, especially where it improves clarity, saves time and supports better decisions. This journal is where I will keep building that reflection over time.
